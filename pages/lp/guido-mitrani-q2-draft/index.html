Platform Operating Layer — Synergy.

StageOperational

Reported before the positions because the institutional case for BOTH as a multi-position platform depends materially on this layer being live and durable. Not a venture to evaluate alongside the others — the operating infrastructure that lets BOTH sustain a four-position book with the process discipline a multi-position fund typically requires multiple times the team size to maintain.

Synergy is a three-tier coordination system. A knowledge layer — every firm meeting recorded, transcribed, and run through an auto-mining pipeline that converts unstructured conversation into structured decisions, risks, and action items, each routed automatically to the right owner and the right venture. A memory layer — a living institutional knowledge base (the "vault"), every position, decision and risk versioned and queryable via semantic search over vector embeddings. An action layer — daily briefs to each principal pulling from calendar, mail, task queue and vault; the weekly partners' meeting runs against a pre-generated digest, not live discovery. The system is deployed as specifications-as-code (harness architecture): every pipeline is an inspectable contract, not opaque automation. Production infrastructure runs on Cloudflare Workers and Supabase Postgres with row-level access controls per principal scope.

The strategic point is institutional, not technological. A multi-position platform — four substantive positions today, more in pipeline — is operationally indefensible without a layer that absorbs the coordination cost. Synergy is the layer. The reason BOTH runs the portfolio that follows without execution drift. The operating moat, not a P&L line.

Quarter contextQ2 milestone: the coordination layer transitioned from sandboxed prototype to firm-wide production, running continuously across all positions. A multi-month architectural bottleneck closed; the system runs as institutional default. That is the right state for operating infrastructure.
Strategic surfaceThe layer is being positioned as institutional intellectual property in its own right, with a published thesis under a Cognitive Architecture frame. Sandbox-first, security audit pre go-live. If executed, Synergy becomes a recognizable institutional voice independent of the portfolio it serves — a separately-valuable asset. Optionality, not commitment.
Cross-portfolio linkThis document was drafted on the platform. Each position section was prepared against living vault pages the system maintains continuously. The quarterly reporting cadence proposed in Section 1 is itself a Synergy deliverable.
Position postureHeld outside the LP commitment. Funded as platform operating expenditure (USD ~650 per month at current load). Reportable as the platform's operating advantage — the layer that makes the multi-position case credible at current team size.

3.4 · b
Side quest — Quantum.
Launch windowQ3 — Q4 2026
Side quest of 3.4ExploreHands-on

The quantum compute direction of Photonic Labs, structured as a sovereign-aligned Uruguay hub through BOTH's working partnership with The Quantum Alliance (TQA, exclusive IonQ LatAm partner). The thesis is straightforward: Latin America does not have an integrated quantum-AI compute hub today; less than 1% of global quantum investment reaches the region; the institutional window to position a country as the regional anchor is open and time-limited.

The opportunity. TQA is building the first distributed advanced-computing network in Latin America — quantum hardware plus AI compute, deployed locally under sovereign control. Buenos Aires is the first central node. Uruguay is the next: structurally suited (stable rule of law, mature multilateral relationships, existing institutional framework for science and innovation) and timed to a market entering its infrastructure deployment phase. The global quantum market is projected at USD 97B by 2035 (McKinsey); USD 40B in annual revenue by 2040 (Quantum Technology Monitor). The window for foundational positioning closes once anchor hubs are placed.

The partnership being built. Working term sheet drafted in Q2 between BOTH and TQA. Two-entity structure: a new Uruguay-domiciled operating entity (NewCo Uruguay) is set up to acquire and operate a 6th-generation 256-qubit IonQ trapped-ion system, with the hardware financed through Uruguayan State plus multilateral (BID-track) instruments. In parallel, BOTH receives a 5% equity stake in TQA HoldCo via an equity swap at strategic discount, plus a USD 10M cash development fee. National contraprestación designed in: training of approximately 1,000 Uruguayan students and technicians. Non-binding until definitive agreements executed.

Q2 progress with the State. Institutional letter to Presidencia ROU prepared with TQA. Two technical reports delivered to support the institutional dialogue: a financing-vias study (BID, multilaterals, sovereign vs. non-sovereign vs. blended/PPP) and a legal-precedent study (Pluna case, state-guarantee structuring exposure). Engagement underway with the relevant Uruguayan agencies (ANII, Uruguay XXI, academic partners) ahead of the public announcement window.

EndgameUruguay positioned as the regional quantum-AI hub. BOTH holds ownership in the local infrastructure asset and an equity position in the broader network.

BOTH carries economics through ownership in the NewCo Uruguay operating entity, the TQA HoldCo stake (5%) and the development fee (USD 10M cash).
