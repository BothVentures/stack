Model 0 — Transversal Base Platform

CAPEX: $500K–$800K | GMP: No | Verdict: ✅ Required in all scenarios

What it does: - Enables all other models without wasted investment - Defensible to investors without confirmed pipeline - Attracts regional startups for analytics services - Covers 60–70% of LATAM biotech project types (CHO, E. coli, yeast, diagnostics)

What it doesn’t do: - No commercial production of any kind - No GMP certification - No clinical lots - Cannot bill for manufacturing

💡 Strategic note: This is the piso inteligente. Invest in Model 0 regardless of which model is chosen above it. It does not depend on strategic decisions not yet made.

Model 3 — R&D + GMP-Ready ⭐ Entry Point

CAPEX: $1M–$1.8M | GMP: Partial (GMP-compatible design) | Verdict: ✅ Recommended Phase 1 target

What it does: - GMP-compatible design from day one: flows, materials, surfaces, drainage - Formal incremental QMS + improved environmental control - Robust technical data for regulatory dossiers and CDMO transfer packages - Positions the lab to jump to full GMP without rebuilding infrastructure - Defensible to investors even without a confirmed pipeline

What it doesn’t do: - No commercial lots - No Phase I–III clinical lot production - No full GMP certification (yet) - Cannot sell direct to end market

💡 Strategic note: Pay for GMP-compatible design today to avoid a costly rebuild tomorrow. The incremental CAPEX over Model 2 is small relative to the rebuild cost avoided. This is the Phase 1 target.

Model 5 — Hybrid (I+D + GMP MVP + Anchor) ⭐⭐ Strategic Target

CAPEX: $2M–$3.5M | GMP: Modular | Verdict: ✅ Steady-state design

What it does: - Anchor tenant guarantees base demand and covers minimum operating costs - Mixed revenue: subscription + CRO fees + GMP service fees + transfer royalties - Modular — grow as demand is confirmed; avoid elefante blanco risk - Royalties / rev-share activate when portfolio products reach market

What it doesn’t do: - Cannot operate safely without anchor without high stranded-asset risk - No mass production from launch - Cannot compete on price with large industrial CDMOs for FDA/EMA commercial lots at scale

💡 Strategic note: Pursue after Model 3 validates the platform and anchor tenant relationship is established. This is the equilibrium model — structurally most balanced under uncertainty.

04 · Business Model

Why this matters: The revenue stack is what makes Photonic Lab financially viable and defensible. No single stream is sufficient. The compounding effect of all five is what creates resilience.

Revenue Stack Architecture

02 Equipment-as-a-Service (EaaS) Transactional Year 1 Per-use pricing Very high

04 Equity participation Asymmetric Year 2+ Warrants / stakes Asymmetric

— Anchor tenant contract Floor revenue Year 2+ Minimum-use contract Medium

Stream Detail

01 · Infrastructure Subscriptions Monthly/annual memberships for lab access, bench space, shared equipment. - Target: 8–15 resident startups at $8K–$20K/month - Anchors operational break-even from day one - Character: predictable, recurring, independent of GMP progress

02 · Equipment-as-a-Service (EaaS) Bioreactors, downstream processing, cleanroom hours, analytical services on demand. - CRO-style pricing for non-resident regional clients - High margin on specialized equipment - Grows with platform reputation in the region

03 · GMP Services + Tech Transfer Fee-for-service GMP lots, regulatory dossier support, CMC packages, tech transfer to CDMOs. - Premium pricing — transforms the platform from lab to development partner - Requires QA Director and regulatory expertise from Phase 2 - Attracts clients from AR/BR/CL/international who cannot afford full CDMO

04 · Equity Participation Warrants or stakes in resident startups in exchange for subsidized access or co-development. - Feeds Manantial.vc deal flow pipeline directly - Asymmetric upside if 1–2 portfolio companies succeed - Aligns platform incentives with startup success

05 · Grants & Public Funding ANII, IDB Lab, CAF, Fondo Sectorial — non-reimbursable capital. - De-risks early phases, reduces equity pressure - Requires outside-ZF entity structure (see Part 05)

Anchor Tenant Contract (Optional, Phase 2+) Minimum-use contract with a regional biotech (Eywa, B4RNA, or equivalent). - Guarantees base revenue floor - Critical if pursuing GMP MVP early (Model 4 gate) - Treat as accelerator, not prerequisite

Recommended Corporate Structure

Entity 1 (outside ZF) — Year 1–3: - Full ANII access - PCTP / UdelaR collaborative research - Flexible international scientific team - Ley Inversiones tax benefits (IRAE exemption, IVA on equipment) - Access to public procurement (ASSE, MSP)

Entity 2 (Zona Franca — Parque de las Ciencias or Zonamerica) — Year 3–4+: - Activated when export revenue exceeds 60% of total - IRAE 0% becomes material at this scale - Import duty savings on equipment - Optimal for regional and international export operations

💡 Two-entity structure adds ~15% admin overhead but maximizes both early public funding access and long-term fiscal efficiency. This is the structure used by the most sophisticated Uruguayan biotechs.

Aesthetic Prompt — Photonic Lab Helvetica Style

One-Line Summary

Swiss editorial modernism: pure white ground, near-black type, a single green accent, and a Helvetica Neue type system with extreme weight contrast — no decoration, no gradients, no shadows beyond the minimum.

Design System

Typography

  • Font stack: "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif — no display fonts, no serif, no monospace
  • Weight system: weight contrast is the primary expressive tool
    • 300 (light) for body copy, subheadings, descriptive text
    • 400 (regular) for secondary labels and nav items
    • 700 (bold) for headlines, labels, key values, active states
  • Letter-spacing: uppercase labels carry heavy tracking (.10em–.18em); headlines carry negative tracking (.025em to .03em)
  • Text-transform: uppercase is used extensively for all metadata, labels, nav items, section eyebrows, and badges — never for body copy
  • Line-height: 1.6 base; 1.75–1.8 for long-form body; .95–1.1 for large display headings
  • Font sizes: strict scale — 8px, 9px, 10px, 11px, 12px, 13px, 14px, 15px for UI and body; clamp(26px, 3.5vw, 40px) for section heads; clamp(48px, 6.5vw, 80px) for the hero H1

Color Palette

All colors are defined as CSS variables. The palette is deliberately minimal:

Geometry & Spacing

  • Border-radius: 3px — minimal rounding, essentially sharp
  • Max layout width: 1160px, centered, 32px horizontal padding
  • Shadow: used only twice: 0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,.08) and 0 2px 12px rgba(0,0,0,.10) — barely perceptible
  • Grid gaps: 1px — grid layouts use background: var(--rule) on the container to render hairline gaps between cells, not gap with color
  • Borders: 1px solid var(--rule) for secondary; 1.5px solid var(--text) for structural (nav, footer, KPI row) — weight distinction is intentional

Component Patterns

Governing Principles

  1. White is the material. The page is white. Every element sits on white or a barely-off-white (#f5f5f3). No dark backgrounds, no glassmorphism, no gradients.
  2. One accent. #0a8c5a is the single primary accent. It appears on active states, eyebrows, key numbers, left-border callouts, and timeline nodes. It is never decorative.
  3. Weight contrast over size contrast. The headline system pairs 300 and 700 on the same type element. This — not color or size alone — creates hierarchy.
  4. Uppercase for metadata, never prose. All labels, badges, nav items, eyebrows, and KPI labels are uppercase. Body copy, card content, and verdict text are sentence case.
  5. Borders do structural work. The nav, KPI row, and footer use 1.5px solid #111110 — heavy black rules act as load-bearing architecture, not decoration.
  6. Hairlines for spatial rhythm. 1px rules in -rule or -rule2 divide sections, table rows, bar rows, and card grid cells. They are the spatial grammar.
  7. No drop shadows except minimum functional ones. Depth is implied by borders and background color shifts, not shadow stacking.
  8. Categorical color is functional. Amber = financial/risk caution, coral = negative/con, blue = capital, slate = ecosystem. These are never used interchangeably or decoratively.
  9. Responsiveness via column collapse. On ≤860px, all multi-column grids collapse to a single column. No complex responsive breakpoint logic — one clean fold.
  10. Radius is nearly zero. 3px border-radius. The aesthetic is rectilinear.
