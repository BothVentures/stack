No-ZF — pros / cons

+ Breaks even ~1 yr sooner · +$160K more grants · full UY uptake + deductible for anchor cohort · COMAP equipment exemption · optionality preserved.

– Pays IRAE once profitable (Y5–6 at full 25%) · COMAP paperwork per equipment.

ZF — pros / cons

+ IRAE 0% forever · auto import exemption · ~$895K tax saved over 6 yr · fits the export endgame (FDA/EMA).

– Benefit back-loaded (worthless pre-profit) · UY clients can't be majority + non-deductible → hits the exact Y1–2 cohort whose LOIs fund the round · no COMAP · deeper start.
★ The elegant solution — the BOTH path (dual-entity, Yr 3–4). Don't choose. Start outside ZF (capture the $400K grants, full UY uptake, faster break-even, survival in the critical window). Then at Year 3–4, when the book turns export-heavy, spin a ZF entity to hold IP and bill foreign clients at 0% IRAE — a non-ZF opco serving UY/regional + a ZF entity for export/IP. Captures both the early grant/uptake advantage and most of the back-end tax saving (~$4.0M cumulative net, the best of either pure path). Both/and, not either/or.
