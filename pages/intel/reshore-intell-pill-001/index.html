The Macro Case —
Why Now Is Real

The structural conditions for Reshore's thesis are not cyclical. They are the product of four simultaneous, reinforcing forces that have been building since 2018 and have now reached a point where companies must act — not plan, not evaluate — act. The window for positioning is open. It will not remain open indefinitely.

The US-China decoupling is no longer a policy debate. It is an operational reality. Tariffs on Chinese goods now exceed 55% on many product categories. The result: a company manufacturing in China for US consumption is paying a structural tax that cannot be engineered away. The only solution is geographic.

Mexico's structural position is strengthening. Its share of US imports is projected to grow from 16.4% to 19% by 2028 (COMCE). The USMCA review in 2026 is creating both urgency and noise — companies need to move now before renegotiation introduces new uncertainty. Reshore operates precisely in this transition window.

"Only 20% of companies plan full reshoring to the US, making Mexico the more cost-efficient alternative."

— Bank of America Global Research, 2025

The nearshoring wave is real but structurally disorganized. Over $170B in FDI commitments to Mexico were announced in the last 18 months. But only 13% was new greenfield investment. The rest was reinvestment or acquisitions — companies moving carefully, not boldly. What they need is infrastructure for trust, discovery, and execution. That infrastructure does not yet exist at scale. That gap is Reshore.

What Reshore Is —
Structural Assessment

Reshore is not a supplier directory. It is not a logistics company. It is not a sourcing agent with software. It is — structurally — an operating system for trust and commerce in Americas manufacturing. That distinction is the investment thesis. The category has never been built. The conditions for building it exist now.

The product architecture reflects this: three interconnected modules (Reshore / Buy / Manufacture), a proprietary 7-step relocation protocol, embedded escrow and financing, and an Intelligence Core that compounds with every transaction. This is not a feature set. It is a learning system — the more it is used, the harder it becomes to replicate.

Fundraising Environment —
Honest Read

LatAm venture is in recovery. 2025 saw a 14.3% increase in total funding ($4.1B across seed through growth), the first significant rebound after three years of correction. But the recovery is structural, not speculative. Capital has returned — but on fundamentally different terms.

The dominant dynamic: capital is priced on execution, not narrative. The 2021 model — vision-first, traction-secondary — no longer works. Investors are running shorter conviction cycles, demanding evidence of repeatability, and requiring governance clarity before committing. For Reshore, this means the pitch must lead with proof, not potential.

"Capital availability has recovered, but it is driven by execution quality and governance maturity, rather than by growth narratives alone. Fewer speculative bets, greater concentration around performing companies with a proven business model."

— LatamList Venture in LatAm Enters 2026, Feb 2026

The specific advantage for Reshore: it sits at the intersection of two investment theses that are both active in the current market — supply chain / industrial tech (US-focused, with Dynamo Fund III freshly deployed) and LatAm B2B infrastructure (NXTP Fund III at $98M, Magma active, 500 LatAm bullish on Mexico). Few companies can credibly claim both. Reshore can.

Narrative Architecture

The narrative problem Reshore faces in fundraising is a framing problem, not a product problem. The product is built. The market is real. What is missing is a story architecture that lands the urgency before the product, and the moat before the market size. Current pitch materials lead with geography rather than pain. That needs to change.

One-liner: "The $315 billion shift of US manufacturing to the Americas is running on spreadsheets and WhatsApp. We are building the operating system for it."

The correct pitch arc moves through five beats in sequence, each one earning the right to the next:

# beat what it establishes current status
01 The Moment — 3 numbers, 30 seconds Urgency. Why this must happen now. Tariffs, lead times, trade volume shift. underweighted
02 The Team — early, with credentials Trust. Ex-Alibaba + Mercado Libre + JP Morgan. Post-exit founders. This unlocks everything. buried
03 The Problem — in dollars and days Pain specificity. 6+ months to rebuild tooling. 35% TCO premium. 100-day lead times. present but weak
04 The Protocol + Platform — proof of system Credibility. A proprietary 7-step process + product demo. Shows it's real, it works. strong
05 The Moat — Intelligence Core explained plainly Long-term defensibility. Every transaction improves the next. Data compounds. undefined in pitch

Three critical framing shifts required in current materials:

  • Infrastructure, not marketplace. Reshore is the trust layer for Americas manufacturing. Marketplace = 3–5x revenue multiple. Infrastructure = 15–20x. The distinction is not semantic. It is the exit story.
  • Team slide needs to be slide 3. Post-exit founders with inside experience at Alibaba and Mercado Libre — the two platforms that defined China-Americas sourcing — is a top-tier credential for this exact thesis. It is currently being undersold.
  • The tariff objection must be pre-empted, not answered. Build a slide that says: "If tariffs stay — companies that haven't moved lose 35%+ margin. If tariffs come down — companies that already moved still save on TCO, lead times, and geopolitical risk. Reshore wins either way."

Round Structure
& Milestones

The round should be structured to close in a single process, not rolled. Runway management and investor signaling both argue against an open-ended raise. Target a firm commitment of $2.5M, with documented capacity to accept up to $4M in oversubscription if investor demand exceeds the floor.

parameter structure
Instrument SAFE (MFN + pro-rata) preferred for speed; priced round if institutional lead requires it
Floor / ceiling $2.5M firm close / $4M maximum with oversubscription
Lead investor One institutional lead (Dynamo or NXTP) writing $750K–$1.5M, setting terms for remaining participants
Round composition Lead + Latitud follow-on + 1–2 Tier 2 VCs ($250–500K each) + 3–5 operator angels ($50–200K each)
Use of funds 40% product/engineering (Intelligence Core) · 35% GTM / US buyer acquisition · 25% ops / manufacturer onboarding
Valuation anchor Nuvocargo raised at ~$30–40M post-seed (comparable US-Mexico trade infrastructure). Reshore has broader platform scope.

Three milestones that unlock the round — in order of impact:

  • 2–3 paying buyer contracts. Even $10–30K ARR with named companies transforms "promising" to "traction." One real paying customer outweighs ten LOIs in the current investor environment.
  • One documented completed relocation. A single full-cycle story — company X moved Y SKUs from China to Mexico in Z weeks, saving $W/month — told in numbers is worth 10 slides of product features. Build this case study now.
  • 10+ verified manufacturers on the platform. Demonstrates supply-side liquidity. Make it visually demonstrable in pitch — live demo or screenshots showing an active, searchable, vetted network.

The round should be treated as a structured sales process with a defined pipeline, parallel tracks, and a hard close date. Urgency is manufactured through parallel investor conversations, not by waiting for one to finish before starting the next.

Build data room (cap table, financial model, product demo video, customer evidence). Define SAFE terms or round structure. Identify 1 reference customer willing to take calls with investors.

Use Tier 1 interest to open Tier 2 conversations with urgency ("we have a term sheet in discussion, filling the round"). Open Magma, FJ Labs, ALLVP conversations. Confirm angel commitments in writing. First close target: $1.5M committed. Begin legal prep for SAFE docs.
