PART I — THE ARBITER'S VERDICT

Stress Test Forum — Verdict

Opportunity: BOTH brings IonQ 256-qubit to LatAm via TQA, in a Uruguay OpCo BOTH controls, ~$50M price (cost ~$18-20M), triple value-capture (OpCo + 5% TQA + $10M fee), state-enabled BID/multilateral financing · North star: best possible deal for BOTH

The Surviving Deal

The attacks converge on a single architectural truth that nearly every seat reached independently, including three of the five teammates: the deal as presented collapses three different things — origination, ownership, and the financed price — into one brokered transaction, and that collapse is simultaneously the legal/reputational kill-switch (Black Swan, Andrés, Daiana) and the value-capture trap (Assassin, Systems). The surviving deal de-fuses it by separation. Concretely:

1. Supply is the floor of everything — gate the letter on it.
No BOTH obligation (Presidency letter, financing close, fee) springs until there is a fully-executed IonQ↔TQA purchase agreement with named unit, fixed date, fixed price (Assassin FATAL, Black Swan SEVERE, Mariano line-in-sand, Gaston's own #1 threat). Until then, either slip the letter or word it to claim only LOI-stage conditional status, with the LOI attached and its status disclosed (Andrés, Rene's "one story," Black Swan). Plus a direct IonQ step-in / assignable SLA to the OpCo on TQA default/change-of-control (Mariano, Assassin, Systems) — the dependency must survive TQA.

2. Three instruments, three separate fund sources — never the financed price as the margin vehicle.
This is Daiana's structure and it is also Andrés's, Black Swan's, Systems', and Rene's, arrived at from five directions:
- (a) An honest-price purchase of the machine at an independent third-party appraised value (Systems, Black Swan, Daiana, Andrés) — price set by someone who does not profit from it.
- (b) 5% TQA equity bought from BOTH's own declared capital/margin, as a clean separate equity line (Daiana, Rene, Andrés) with anti-dilution + an info/governance right over network pricing (Systems).
- (c) BOTH's value capture as a defined arm's-length 5-year operating/services contract (~$12M, UBA comparable), paid from OpCo operating REVENUE not financing proceeds, billed over time so every disclosure shows work performed (Systems, Andrés, Rene, Gaston's own full-YES reframe, Daiana). The word "comisión" dies — unanimous across the four teammates who addressed compensation.
- The $10M cash fee in its original form does not survive. It is the single most-attacked term in the room.

3. Capitalize INTO the OpCo directly — don't let money "go and come back."
Open Quantum Uruguay SA and raise capital into it (Daiana), structured as a repayable concessional loan to a private OpCo, not a government grant (Black Swan, Andrés, Rene) — so a change of government cannot recast it as gifted public money, and there is no destination-of-funds duty to violate. Use-of-proceeds must match stated destination (Andrés: the Pluna failure mode) — if the envelope is tagged "infrastructure + training," no BOTH fee may surface inside it.

4. Disclose first, voluntarily, in writing.
A single version-controlled Due Diligence Disclosure Pack (full cap-table, money-flow diagram, LOI-with-status, related-party disclosure), a conflict-of-interest disclosure as a signed instrument countersigned by the State before commitment (Andrés, Daiana, Gaston's "todo arriba de la mesa"), and MDB-integrity pre-clearance — a written opinion from MDB-qualified counsel before submission (Black Swan). Rene's frame governs: publish the structure ourselves before anyone scratches.

5. De-risk operations and the network position before signing.
- Binding O&M agreement with IonQ (named on-site headcount, response SLA + penalties, uptime guarantee, multi-year price) signed before purchase, plus a funded operating reserve for years 1-3 carved from the financing envelope (Rebuilder FATAL, Mariano). BOTH is originator + equity holder, not operator-of-record (Rebuilder).
- Lock network-settlement economics NOW: a fixed core-node take-rate on all inter-hub consumption, with a floor, surviving TQA change-of-control (Systems SEVERE, Mariano) — and auditable orchestration + data-plane co-ownership held by the OpCo (Mariano: "core is a software position, not a location").
- TQA take-or-pay floor to the OpCo sized to cover debt service + O&M regardless of downstream hubs (Rebuilder SEVERE).
- Contraprestación (train ~1,000 technicians) made TQA's funded contractual deliverable, or if BOTH owns it, priced + owned + carved from the envelope, before the letter (Rebuilder).

6. Reconcile the price internally before it leaves the building.
The $50M/$30M/$20M discrepancy is reconciled to one number across every document before anything reaches Presidencia (Assassin, Systems, Daiana, Andrés). Do not fix a number "because it stuck from a joke."

Conflicts between neutralizing terms, named not silently resolved:
- OpCo control: 100% (Gaston) vs 50%+governance (Rene, Systems). Gaston wants economic majority and will not be a minority on the entity owning the machine; Rene argues 50%-economic-plus-governance-control is narratively cleaner ("stewardship vs capture") and Systems wants majority economic modeled but flags that control on a state-financed asset feeds the self-dealer frame. Unresolved — see LIVE DISAGREEMENTS.
- Compensation paid from REVENUE (Andrés, Systems) vs the milestone-obligation instinct (Assassin's "non-refundable fee payable on signing of state interest"). Andrés is explicit: paid from OpCo operating revenue, NOT financing proceeds. The Assassin's capture-it-early term would route value through exactly the flow Black Swan says triggers debarment. The teammate position wins on merits here — but it means BOTH waits longer for its money, which is real exposure if the deal slips.

  • Aggressive capture ↔ exposure (the core team fracture). Gaston wants all three legs unapologetically — OpCo 100% / 50%+governance fallback, 5% TQA, $10M cash fee, price fixed at $50M, letter to Presidencia before Friday, "fix now negotiate down later," BID grant ("la de máxima"). Note: Gaston values Daiana holding the line, and his own full-YES (reframe the fee, swap to services contract, hand the stack to the IFC) already concedes most of her structure — the live gap is speed and the grant-vs-loan question, not whether to clean it up.

  • OpCo control: 100% economic (Gaston) vs 50%-economic-plus-governance (Rene + Systems). Gaston: "will NOT be a minority on the entity that owns the machine." Rene: co-holding economic ownership with the region is the cleaner headline — "stewardship vs capture." Systems wants majority economic modeled. Unresolved because it's a genuine value-vs-narrative trade with no dominant answer, and it directly changes how the integrity reviewer (Threat 1) reads the deal.

  • Grant vs loan — and Gaston's "la de máxima" grant instinct vs the entire risk wing. Gaston tops the cascade with the non-dilutive BID grant. Black Swan, Andrés, Daiana, and Rene all say the grant is the trap — a grant creates a destination-of-funds duty (Pluna's exact failure) and lets a change of government recast it as gifted public money. A repayable concessional loan to a private OpCo is the safer instrument. This is an adversary-and-teammate bloc directly contradicting the CEO's stated preference. It does not average away: the loan is structurally safer; the grant is cheaper capital. Gaston's full-YES says "financed by the BID grant" — this is the single sharpest unreconciled point between his position and four other seats.

  • Capture early (Assassin) vs capture from revenue over time (Andrés/Systems). The Assassin wants the fee locked as a non-refundable milestone payable on signing of state interest, independent of completion — to beat TQA's decaying-leverage problem. Andrés/Systems want compensation paid from OpCo operating revenue over 5 years, after work is performed — to beat the integrity finding. These are directly opposed remedies to two different FATAL threats (capture-loss vs debarment). Flagged: the revenue-over-time path leaves BOTH exposed if the deal slips after BOTH has spent its Presidency access.

  • Is the asset the box or the position? Gaston/Daiana frame the crown jewel as owning the company that holds the machine. Mariano and Systems say the box is a depreciating sunk asset and the orchestration/network-settlement layer is the real position — and it's currently TQA's. Rene splits the difference: "the fee and 5% are plumbing, ownership is the headline." Unresolved because if Mariano is right, 100% of a box while TQA owns the network rules is the worse outcome than 50% of a properly-architected position.

No conformity-artifact flag: this was a single-round forum with no cross-examination, so no stance-softening to check.

The Question Nobody Answered

Why does TQA need BOTH at all — and what stops IonQ from doing this deal with Uruguay directly? Every seat attacked BOTH's position relative to TQA. Nobody asked the layer beneath: TQA itself holds only an LOI. If the asset is "first commercial-scale quantum in LatAm via a sovereign relationship BOTH originates," then IonQ — the actual owner of the only scarce, durable asset (the machine and the queue slot) — is one introduction away from realizing it does not need TQA or BOTH. The entire deal assumes IonQ will transact through a two-layer broker chain (IonQ→TQA→BOTH→State) rather than collapsing it. No seat tested the disintermediation risk from the top of the stack. BOTH may be the second-most-replaceable party, not the first — and the most-replaceable is TQA.

Verdict

RESTRUCTURE THEN REVISIT. The deal as presented (single brokered transaction, $10M cash fee inside a sovereign-financed inflated price, grant financing, letter before Friday) does not survive its own room — three teammates and three adversaries independently route to the same kill-switch. But a real deal survives: gate everything on a signed IonQ PO + step-in; three instruments from three fund sources; capitalize into the OpCo as a repayable loan; voluntary disclosure with MDB pre-clearance; one signed Uruguayan offtake before fixing the price. That deal is worth pursuing. The current one is not — and the difference is structure, not nerve. Gaston's own full-YES already describes most of the surviving deal; the restructure is mainly forcing his Friday/grant instinct to yield to the supply-gate and the loan-not-grant bloc.
