1. Messaging

Hero Headline

Current: "Your Gateway to Vetted Manufacturing in Mexico"

Issues:

  • Too geographic, too narrow. Limiting the value prop to "Mexico" contradicts their broader "Americas" positioning used throughout the rest of the page. This immediately filters out prospects who may want US-only or multi-country nearshoring.
  • "Gateway" is vague and passive. A "gateway" is not an outcome — it's infrastructure. The headline doesn't tell visitors what they'll achieve. Visitors want a result, not a door.
  • The subheadline ("Relocate your production with confidence through pre-qualified partners") is cleaner and outcome-oriented, but is undercut by the generic word "confidence."

Jargon & Wording Issues

"Agentic-commerce lifecycle" — opaque B2B jargon

No procurement or supply chain manager searches for "agentic-commerce." It's an internal product metaphor, not customer language.

Fix: Replace with "How ongoing sourcing works after you're set up"

"Intelligence Core" — undefined internal brand

Mentioned multiple times but never explained in plain terms. Visitors don't know what it means until they've read the entire page — and by then, most have left.

Fix: Add a one-line definition on first use: "AI that learns from every transaction to improve matching, pricing, and fraud detection."

"Agentic Intelligence" — conflated with product name

Used as both a product feature name and a general descriptor ("agentic OS", "agentic commerce lifecycle"). This dilutes the brand term.

✅ "The Reshoring Protocol" — strong brand term

Memorable, concrete, and differentiated. It implies a system with steps. Works well and should be leveraged further.

ICP (Ideal Customer) Clarity

The page speaks to at least three different audiences without segmenting them:

  1. US Buyers / Brands — wanting to move production out of China (primary audience based on hero)
  2. Procurement / Ops teams — who manage supplier relationships and RFQ processes
  3. Mexican Manufacturers — "Grow your business" section targets them directly, buried near the bottom

Critical issue: Mixing buyer and manufacturer messaging on a single page creates cognitive dissonance. A US buyer reading "Get discovered by verified US buyers" mid-page may think the platform is for suppliers — causing drop-off at a critical scroll depth.

Critical CTA Issues

"Join the Waitlist" signals product unavailability

The hero CTA — the highest-value real estate on the entire page — tells visitors the product isn't ready. This is the single most damaging conversion issue on the page.

Fix: Replace with "Book a Free Relocation Assessment" or "Start Your Reshoring Plan"

No secondary CTA pathway

High-intent visitors (ready to buy) and low-intent visitors (just researching) need different paths. There's only one pathway — a single contact form. Early-stage researchers have no "learn more without committing" option.

Fix: Add a secondary CTA such as "Download the Reshoring Guide" or "See how the protocol works" (linking to a demo video)

CTA text is all lowercase

"learn more," "explore our platform," "join reshore" — may be a brand style choice, but all-lowercase can underperform sentence-case or caps in A/B tests for readability and perceived authority.

What's Working

✅ Brand name "Reshore" is exceptional

Exact-match keyword, memorable, action-verb as noun. Instantly communicates what the company does. The domain reshore.com is a significant competitive asset.

✅ "The Reshoring Protocol" is a strong proprietary framework

Naming your process builds authority. It implies rigor, repeatability, and expertise. Should be used as a lead magnet name or guide title.

✅ Visual tone feels premium and tech-forward

Clean, minimal, credible for an enterprise B2B audience.

Brand Issues

🔴 Zero trust infrastructure

No customer logos. No testimonials. No case studies. No team bios. No press mentions. "Backed by investors" with no names is actively worse than nothing — it implies the investors don't want to be associated publicly. For a company asking brands to trust them with production relocation, this is critical.

Tone oscillates between technical and visionary

"The Reshoring Protocol" (methodical, operational) sits next to "Building the future of Americas trade" (visionary, aspirational). The site can't decide if it's a consulting firm or a tech platform.

Fix: Pick a lane. Operational precision with tech-enabling backstory, OR technology-first with operational proof. Don't alternate paragraph by paragraph.

Proprietary terms introduced without anchoring

"Intelligence Core," "Buyer Copilot," "Manufacturer Command Center," "Agentic OS" — these brand terms appear without one-line definitions. Visitors can't tell if these are product names, feature names, or copywriting metaphors.

Critical SEO Issues

🔴 Title tag is just "Reshore©" — near-zero SEO value

The page cannot rank for any non-branded query. For a company sitting on reshore.com, this is a massive missed opportunity.

Fix: "Reshore | Nearshore Manufacturing in Mexico & Americas | AI-Powered Reshoring Platform"

🔴 No meta description

Search engines will auto-generate one from page content, likely pulling confusing or off-brand text.

Fix: "Reshore helps US companies move manufacturing from China to Mexico and the Americas — without rebuilding from scratch. AI-powered sourcing, vetted partners." (155 chars)

🔴 H1 targets the wrong keywords

"Your Gateway to Vetted Manufacturing in Mexico" — "vetted manufacturing" has nearly zero search volume. Target keywords: "nearshore manufacturing," "Mexico manufacturing," "reshoring from China," "contract manufacturing Mexico."

High-Value Keywords Not Targeted

High volume, commercial intent:

  • nearshore manufacturing Mexico
  • reshoring from China to US
  • contract manufacturing nearshore
  • Mexico manufacturing partner
  • US-Mexico supply chain

Lower volume, high conversion:

  • plastic injection molding Mexico
  • tooling transfer China to Mexico
  • mold relocation service
  • nearshore sourcing platform
  • manufacturer RFQ Mexico

Opportunities

Content moat: "The Reshoring Protocol" as a full guide

Publishing the protocol as a 5,000+ word SEO-optimized article could rank for "how to reshore manufacturing," "reshoring checklist," "moving production from China" — high-intent queries from exactly Reshore's target customer. The content already exists in outline form on the page.

Framer / JS rendering risk

The animated ticker with plastic manufacturing categories may not be fully indexed by Google. Critical keyword-bearing text should exist in static HTML, not only in JS-rendered components.
